Project & P3 is about implementing change

  • Seeks to introduce change
  • Limited by time
  • Teams working with unique/bespoke plans & risks
  • Specified scope so we understand what the project work & products are
  • Produce specific one-off deliverables
  • They have discrete number of steps (delineated by the finite time), called a Project Life-cycle
  • Projects seek step change and transformation
  • Requires a specific authorised business case

Business as Usual is about managing routine activities

  • Seeks to maintain a stable platform for efficient production
  • Repetitive and continues indefinitely
  • Highly procedural working practices to enable complex operations to be delivered in a complex way
  • The first few prototypes of a mass-produced item may be a project; once production is on-going, they become Business as Usual
  • Produce specific deliverables, but repeatedly
  • Products go through a life-cycle from build, through operations to disposal, called a Product Life-cycle
  • Business as Usual seeks continuity, consistency & slow incremental improvement
  • Normally funded from operational budgets rather than capital budgets, business cases are less relevant
