Advantages

  • Teams are formed and often co-located, which means team members physically work at the same location.
  • Project team members report to the project manager, not to a functional or departmental manager.
  • Project managers have high to ultimate authority over the project.
  • Loyalties are formed to the project, not to a functional manager.
  • Clear line of authority enables team members directly report to the project manager.
  • Reduced conflicts and faster decision making.
  • Due to a single reporting system, there is strong and effective communication within the project management team.
  • Due to a single authority, less time is consumed in communication, and the response to stakeholdersâ€™ concerns is fast.

Disadvantages

  • Project team members might find themselves out of work at the end of the project. An example of this might be a consultant who works on a project until completion and then is put on the bench or let go at the end of the project.
  • If you have a situation where you need a highly specialized skill at certain times throughout the project, the resource you're using to perform this function might be idle during other times in the project. Hence managing costs & expectations get difficult.
  • Project Manager has too much Authority.
  • Functioning is characterized by strict deadlines and tight schedules, which makes the work environment stressful.
