• The governance of P3 management requires a permanent organisational infrastructure that supports the temporary organisational structures of projects, programmes and portfolios.
  • The project management office (PMO) is usually a centralized organizational unit that oversees the management of projects and programs throughout the organization.
  • Many different names are given to the structures that ‘own’ P3 management within an organisation.
  • The terms EPMO (Enterprise Project Management Office), PSO (Project, Programme or Portfolio Support Office), Project Services or Centre of Excellence are all in common use.
  • The name often reflects the scope of responsibilities. Some PMOs may have a great deal of authority and control, whereas others may only serve a supporting role.
  • For example, a Project Support Office will provide predominantly administrative support to a project, while a Centre of Excellence will often be responsible for improving P3 management maturity.
  • Additionally, a support officer might provide administrative support to a specific project or programme. This is then disbanded once the work is complete. In contrast, a community of practice, or centre of excellence, has a permanent support role independent of the creation and completion of any individual piece of work.
